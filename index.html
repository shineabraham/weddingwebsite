A Celebration of Heritage

Our Knanaya Traditions

We are so proud to celebrate our wedding in the spirit of our Knanaya roots—a community that has cherished a unique blend of faith and culture for generations. Our traditions are a beautiful reflection of our history, valuing the deep bonds of family and the sacredness of marriage.

Throughout our celebration, you'll see special rituals that have been passed down through the centuries. On Day 1, we begin with the engagement ceremony in the church, followed by a reception where we also have Mylanchi Ideel (henna ceremony for the bride) and Chantham Charthal (Grooms Pre-wedding rituals). These customs aren't just rituals; they are joyful moments of prayer, song, and community that mark the beginning of our new life together.

Whether these traditions are familiar to you or you are experiencing them for the first time, we are so honoured to share them with you. Get ready for a celebration full of heart, music, and the legendary Knanaya spirit of hospitality!

These celebrations are relaxed and inclusive — there is no right or wrong way to join in. You'll be gently guided through everything on the day, and all that's asked of you is your presence and your joy.

Getting Here

Everything you need to know to make your way to Kottayam, Kerala

Cochin International Airport (COK) is the recommended arrival point — approximately 70 km from Kottayam (~1.5 hrs by road).

Trivandrum International Airport (TRV) is an alternative (~150 km, ~3 hrs).

On arrival, pick up a Jio or Airtel tourist SIM at the arrivals hall — staff set it up on the spot, and data in India is wonderfully affordable.

Kottayam Railway Station is a major stop on the Ernakulam–Thiruvananthapuram rail corridor with excellent connections from across India.

For your convenience and flexibility, we recommend using the Prepaid Taxi counter located just before the airport exit. It is the most reliable way to travel to your accommodation, especially for late-night arrivals.

How it works: No pre-booking is needed. Simply head to the counter, state your destination, and pay a fixed fare.

Wait time: Usually under 15 minutes.

Estimated Fares:
• Compact Sedan: ~₹2,400 (~£23 / ~$27)
• SUV: ~₹3,600 (~£34 / ~$40)

Note: While Ola and Uber operate in Kochi and Kottayam, the airport prepaid counter is generally the quickest and most straightforward option.

Taxis, Ola & Uber are readily available throughout Kottayam and Kumarakom for your stay.

Wedding shuttles will run between the church and reception venues on both days — no logistics to worry about once you're here.

Most international guests will need an e-Tourist Visa to enter India — apply online. We recommend applying at least 2 weeks before travel to be safe.

See the Questions & Answers section below for a nationality-by-nationality breakdown. Always verify the latest requirements with your embassy or the official Indian e-Visa portal.

We recommend arriving by 2nd January 2027 or earlier to celebrate New Year's in Kochi and to settle in before the celebrations begin. Departing on 6th January or later gives you time to soak in the beauty of Kerala.

Questions & Answers

Where are the ceremonies taking place?

Day 1 (3rd Jan) — Engagement ceremony at Aramana Palli, Kottayam, followed by the engagement reception at Windsor Castle, Kodimatha. Day 2 (5th Jan) — Marriage ceremony at Aramana Palli, followed by the wedding reception at Backwater Ripples, Kumarakom.

What should we expect during the church ceremony?

The Knanaya Catholic ceremony runs for approximately one hour and is a beautiful, dignified occasion — nothing to be nervous about!

Sitting and standing: The congregation will rise and be seated at key moments throughout the service. Simply follow those around you and you'll be perfectly in step.

Language: The ceremony is conducted in Malayalam, with some English elements. The atmosphere is warm and the meaning of each moment will be felt regardless of language.

Holy Communion: When the congregation goes forward to receive Communion, non-Catholics are warmly welcome to remain seated — this is completely normal and respected. You may also go forward with arms crossed over your chest to receive a blessing instead, if you'd like to participate in that moment.

Photography: Please follow any guidance from the priest or ushers on the day regarding photos during the ceremony.

Come as you are, bring your warmth, and leave the rest to us.

Will there be transportation between venues?

Yes! A shuttle bus will be available from the church to the reception venue on both days. Details will be shared closer to the event.

What is the weather like in January?

January is one of the best months to visit Kerala — warm, sunny days of 24–32°C (75–90°F) with low humidity. Light, breathable fabrics are your best friend. Evenings by the backwaters can be pleasantly cool, so a light layer is worth packing.

Currency, SIM cards and practical tips for India

Currency: Indian Rupees (INR). ATMs are widely available in Kottayam town and most hotels accept cards.

SIM cards: Airtel and Jio tourist SIMs are available at the Kochi airport arrivals hall — staff will set it up on the spot, and data in India is wonderfully affordable.

Drinking water: Tap water isn't recommended for drinking; sealed bottled water is available at every hotel, restaurant, and corner shop.

What is there to do in Kochi if we arrive early (Jan 1–2)?

You're in luck — Kochi is one of India's most characterful cities and January is the best time to explore it.

Fort Kochi & Mattancherry — The Chinese fishing nets at sunrise are iconic. Walk through Mattancherry's lanes to the Dutch Palace, then lose an hour in the antique shops and spice stalls of Jew Town. It's a 2–3 hour walk that costs almost nothing and stays with you forever.

The Harbour Ferry — Take the public KSINC ferry from Fort Kochi jetty to Ernakulam for under ₹10. Harbour views are beautiful; the return trip at golden hour is especially so.

Where to eat — Dhe Puttu for a proper Kerala breakfast (puttu, kadala curry, chai). Malabar Junction in Fort Kochi for excellent coastal Kerala cuisine. Kayees Rahmathulla Hotel in Mattancherry for Malabar biryani that regulars swear by.

New Year's Day note — The city is festive and lively on Jan 1st. Some government-run monuments like the Dutch Palace may be closed, but the waterfront, streets, and restaurants are fully open. Marine Drive along the harbour is a lovely evening stroll.

What should we do on January 4th (the day between celebrations)?

Jan 4th is entirely yours — tucked between the Engagement (Jan 3) and the Wedding (Jan 5), it's a lovely day to exhale and explore.

• Alleppey houseboat or shikara ride (~45 mins from Kottayam) — A slow float through the backwaters. Book a half-day shikara if a full overnight isn't your style.
• Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary (~30 mins) — Migratory birds fill the sanctuary in winter. A peaceful morning walk before the heat sets in.
• Kottayam's ancient churches — Valiya Palli and Cheria Palli date to the 1600s and sit side by side in town. Among the oldest Syrian Christian churches in India — beautiful whether or not you're religious.

Or simply rest. Two big celebrations flank this day, and there is no shame in enjoying the hotel, the pool, and a very long Kerala lunch.

What language is spoken locally?

Malayalam is the local language of Kerala, but English is widely spoken in hotels, restaurants, and at both our venues. You'll be well looked after!

What should I see while in Kerala?

Kerala is truly God's Own Country! Consider a houseboat journey through the Alleppey backwaters, the misty tea plantations of Munnar, or the golden beaches of Varkala. A morning cruise from Kumarakom is an experience you won't forget.
